About Me

Background

I have worked in a number of roles across food and farming, and the technology sector. I am interested in the role of technologies in promoting equality and diversity in social systems, and in enabling a transition to more sustainable food system.

Current Position

In February 2022, I began learning full-stack web development on the Netmatters Scion Coalition Scheme (SCS). My interests are in understanding how technologies can enable a transition to a sustainable and equitable food system, and how the technology sector can redress structural inequalities in society. I have experience working within the technology sector, first in a business support role at limber. and subsequently as project lead at Cookpad, on a project developing digital tools to enable sustainable farming.

I began training in software development for a number reasons: to improve my understanding of the technical aspects of coding, to gain practical skills I could apply in my own learning, and to improve the quality of my conversations with software developers. Having worked within technology companies before, but without an intimate knowledge of how technologies are built, I felt that increasing my understanding of coding and the software development process would improve my ability to collaborate with developers.

On completion of the SCS course, I hope to find work with a technology company that is actively engaging with the issues of sustainability, climate change, and diversity and equality in technology and society.

Education and Qualifications

I studied veterinary science for 3 years at the University of Bristol, before transferring onto the BSc in animal behaviour and welfare, graduating in 2016 (1st class honours).

I have a further 7 months' experience in higher education, as a PhD student studying sustainable agricultural sciences at Rothamsted Research and the University of Bristol.

Coding Experience

I first began coding during my PhD studies, when I began learning (to a very beginner level): Visual Basic for Excel, Unix and Perl for bioinformatics, and R for statistics.

Whilst working at limber., I learned how to query the company's databases using Google BigQuery and SQL.

Prior to starting the SCS course, I was using online platforms to learn Python, Ruby, and the fundamental principles of computer science.

Additional Training

  • Anti-Racist Approaches in Technology, UAL Creative Computing Institute & Coding Black Females (in progress)
